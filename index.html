Welcome to The Circle.

In most cases, when you study a language solely through grammar, it often feels foreign, distant, and rigid; and when it’s not your native language, you feel embarrassed when trying to speak it or express yourself. This happens in part because you’re approaching the language solely through reason. But in this course, we know that the greatest learning isn’t just centered on reason or thought but explores the sensory, emotions, and personal experience.

After Mexico, the United States is the country with the second-largest number of Spanish speakers in the world, which reveals that it is a language deeply connected to our community and to the way of life and feeling in this country. Spanish in the United States is not just grammar; it is heritage, family, emotion, passion, and a force that unites us in difficult times and, by its very nature, links us to all the peoples of Latin America, the Caribbean, and parts of Europe. In this sense, the new ways of feeling, expressing, and living become almost infinite in the Spanish language.

El Circulo is a self-reinforcing path of growth through language learning and experience. This course follows a circular path for experimenting with and practicing new meanings and growth in the Spanish language. The method is simple: Observe (the text), Listen (to the audio), and finally Write (your reflections in words), and the learning cycle begins again. With each turn of the circle, you will experience growth.

The course lasts four weeks with three exercises each week, and we will explore texts by contemporary Latin American authors: Juan Villoro, Clyo Mendoza, Yuri Herrera, Mónica Ojeda, and Fernanda Melchor. In this first template, you will find the first exercises for each week for free. If you wish to join the Circle, you can visit our Patreon community where the learning continues.

Syllabus of Learning

Selecciona una semana para iniciar el Círculo

Week 1 Juan Villoro I

"Los Culpables" Part I. Descubre la cadencia, el humor sutil y la tensión conversacional en la escritura de Juan Villoro.

Week 2 Juan Villoro II

"Los Culpables" Part IV. Continuamos el viaje auditivo desmenuzando los detalles más profundos e íntimos del cuanto de Villoro.

Week 3 Mendoza & Herrera

"Furia" / Selections. Adéntrate en la atmósfera poética del desierto de Clyo Mendoza y los paisajes biculturales de Yuri Herrera.

Week 4 Ojeda & Melchor

"Las Voladoras" / Selections. Explora el lirismo del gótico andino de Mónica Ojeda y el realismo tenso veracruzano de Fernanda Melchor.

Hola. Soy el creador de El Círculo. Tras años de trabajar con comunidades bilingües y estudiantes de herencia mexicana en Estados Unidos, diseñé este espacio de aprendizaje para practicar y sentir el Español como una lengua viva. Mi objetivo es ayudarte a generar tu propia dinámica circular de aprendizaje a rítmica. Mi B.A en Filosofía y mi M.A. in Latin American Literatures and Cultures por NEIU no son solo títulos académicos sino que han sido fruto del contacto directo con la cultura, las artes, el cine, la música, la poesía y el lenguaje de nuestras comunidades en Chicago. Actualmente soy director de la plataforma cultural Xochipilli Culture, y dialogo con artistas de la diáspora latinoamericana en nuestro Podcast y espacios de radio comunitaria. Si tienes alguna duda sobre las prácticas o deseas participar en un taller presenciales, escríbeme directamente.
